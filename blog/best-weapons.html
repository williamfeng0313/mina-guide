Best Weapons in Mina the Hollower: Which Weapon Should You Use?

The best weapon in Mina the Hollower depends on what you are trying to do. A weapon that feels great for bosses may feel risky during exploration, while a safer weapon may be better for beginners or 100% cleanup.

Quick Answer

  • Best beginner weapon: the safer, faster weapon with reliable reach.
  • Best high-damage weapon: Hammer, if you already understand spacing.
  • Best boss weapon: the weapon that lets you punish safely after patterns.
  • Best exploration weapon: a consistent weapon that avoids contact damage.
  • Best 100% cleanup weapon: the one that keeps you alive while revisiting old areas.

Best Weapon for Beginners

For a first playthrough, the best weapon is usually not the one with the highest damage. It is the weapon that lets you attack without constantly taking contact damage.

Mina rewards spacing, patience, and knowing when not to swing. If you are still learning enemy movement, a safer and faster weapon is usually better than a heavy weapon that locks you into risky attacks.

Beginner weapon checklist

  • Can you hit enemies without standing too close?
  • Can you recover quickly after missing?
  • Can you safely attack small or fast enemies?
  • Can you use it while learning boss patterns?

If the answer is yes, that weapon is probably a better first choice than raw damage.

Is Hammer a Good Starting Weapon?

Hammer can be strong, but it is not very forgiving early. Its damage is useful, but the shorter range and slower timing can make new players take more contact damage.

Hammer feels better once you already understand enemy spacing. If you know when enemies commit to an attack, Hammer can punish hard. If you are still learning the game, it can feel clumsy and dangerous.

Use Hammer if:

  • You are comfortable baiting enemy attacks.
  • You do not panic-swing at close range.
  • You know when it is safe to commit.
  • You want higher damage and can accept more risk.

Avoid Hammer early if:

  • You keep taking contact damage.
  • You are still learning enemy spacing.
  • You prefer safer attacks during exploration.
  • You want a smoother first playthrough.

In short: Hammer is not bad. It is just less beginner-friendly.

Best Weapon for Boss Fights

Boss fights in Mina are less about constant attacking and more about clean punishment. The best boss weapon is the one that lets you hit safely after a pattern ends.

If a weapon makes you chase damage and trade hits, it is probably hurting you more than helping you. A slower weapon can work well if you are patient, but it becomes risky if you try to force attacks during unsafe windows.

Boss weapon rule

Choose the weapon that gives you the most consistent safe hits, not the weapon with the biggest theoretical damage.

Best Weapon for Exploration and Cleanup

Exploration is different from boss fighting. During cleanup, you are often revisiting old areas, dealing with small enemies, awkward rooms, hazards, and backtracking.

For this part of the game, consistency matters more than burst damage.

For exploration, prioritize:

  • Safe range
  • Fast recovery
  • Low contact damage risk
  • Comfort in repeated rooms
  • Reliability against small enemies

How to Think About Starting Weapons

The starting weapon choice matters because it shapes your early-game comfort. A high-risk weapon can make the opening hours feel harder than they need to be, especially if you are still learning how Mina handles movement and enemy spacing.

If you are unsure, choose the weapon that feels safest and easiest to control. You can experiment with heavier or riskier weapons later, once you understand the game better.

Simple starting weapon advice

  • New player: choose safety and consistency.
  • Confident player: try higher damage options.
  • Completion player: use whatever makes cleanup less annoying.
  • Boss-focused player: pick the weapon that matches your punish timing.

Best Weapon for 100% Completion

For 100% completion, the best weapon is not always the strongest weapon. It is the weapon that keeps you consistent during cleanup.

When you are hunting missed trinkets, revisiting rooms, checking hidden paths, and clearing leftover collectibles, dying to random contact damage wastes more time than low damage does.

Best 100% cleanup mindset

  • Use the weapon you can control safely.
  • Do not chase damage in messy rooms.
  • Switch weapons if a section feels awkward.
  • Prioritize survival during backtracking.

FAQ

What is the best weapon in Mina the Hollower?

There is no single best weapon for every player. For beginners, safety and consistency matter most. For experienced players, higher-damage weapons can be stronger if used well.

Is Hammer bad in Mina the Hollower?

Hammer is not bad. It hits hard, but it can feel rough early because of its shorter range and slower timing. It is better once you understand spacing.

Which weapon should beginners use?

Beginners should usually use the weapon that feels safest, fastest, and easiest to control. Avoid picking purely based on damage.

What weapon is best for 100% cleanup?

The best cleanup weapon is the one that helps you avoid unnecessary damage while revisiting areas and checking missed items.

Should I switch weapons for bosses?

Yes, if another weapon gives you safer punish windows. Boss fights reward clean timing more than blind aggression.
