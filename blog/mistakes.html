Beginner and Progression Guide

10 Common Mina the Hollower Mistakes

Mina the Hollower becomes much easier when you stop losing progress to preventable mistakes. The most damaging errors are rarely about reaction speed alone. They usually involve poor resource management, ignoring NPC progress, using the wrong build for the situation, or attempting cleanup without a reliable tracking method.

Last updated: July 21, 2026

Quick Answer

The biggest mistakes are carrying valuable resources into risky encounters, spending Joules without a plan, ignoring checkpoints and return visits, refusing to change equipment for a boss, and assuming that a missed item is permanently lost. Most of these problems can be fixed by preparing before difficult rooms and keeping a simple completion checklist.

1Carrying Valuable Resources into a Risky Encounter

Entering a boss room, difficult route, or unfamiliar region while carrying a large amount of valuable currency is an avoidable risk. Even when the exact death penalty changes with your current state, spending or securing resources before a dangerous attempt reduces the cost of failure.

The problem is not simply “having too many Bones.” The real mistake is failing to decide whether the next ten minutes are a farming trip, an exploration trip, or a boss attempt.

Better approach: Buy upgrades, visit vendors, or secure your progress before entering an area where repeated deaths are likely.

2Spending Joules Without a Plan

Sidearms are useful during both exploration and boss fights, so using them outside a boss arena is not automatically a mistake. The mistake is spending Joules on low-value situations and then reaching a more dangerous room without the resource needed for control, burst damage, or a safer opening.

A Sidearm should solve a specific problem: hitting a distant target, controlling a crowd, interrupting pressure, or creating damage during a safe window.

Better approach: Decide what your Sidearm is for before entering a region, and avoid using it simply because the resource bar is full.

3Passing a Checkpoint Without Activating It

Progression becomes much more punishing when you walk past an obvious checkpoint, mirror, or safe return point and continue deeper into an unfamiliar route. The next death may force you to repeat several rooms that could have been skipped.

Players sometimes delay interacting with a checkpoint because they want to keep moving or assume they will return later. That saves only a few seconds and can cost several minutes.

Better approach: Activate travel and checkpoint systems when you first reach them, unless the game clearly gives you a reason not to.

4Failing to Revisit NPCs and Shops

NPC dialogue, shop inventory, upgrade paths, and side objectives can change as the story advances. A character who had nothing useful earlier may offer a new conversation, item, or progression step after a major event.

This is especially easy to miss when the game does not place a large quest marker over every change.

Better approach: After major bosses, Sparks, or story milestones, revisit important hubs and speak to recurring NPCs again.

5Using the Same Build for Every Situation

A comfortable general-purpose setup is useful, but it should not become a rule. Bosses, platforming routes, crowded rooms, and cleanup runs reward different priorities.

A high-damage setup may feel excellent in a short boss fight but frustrating during exploration. A defensive setup may keep you alive while slowing down an encounter where aggression is safer.

Better approach: Keep at least three mental presets: boss damage, safe exploration, and 100% cleanup. Use the Build Planner to save combinations.

6Judging a Trinket in Isolation

Many Trinkets look weak or strong when read alone, but their value depends on the weapon, Sidearm, resource loop, and challenge in front of you. Extra capacity is not useful without generation. A defensive effect is less valuable when the encounter is already easy to avoid.

This is why tier lists should be treated as starting points rather than universal rules.

Better approach: Evaluate the complete build. Ask what each Trinket contributes and whether two items are solving the same problem unnecessarily.

7Assuming a Missed Item Has Ruined the Save

Missing an item during its first opportunity can feel like a permanent failure, but some rewards can be recovered later through shops, return visits, alternate conditions, or cleanup routes.

Restarting immediately without checking the exact item wastes far more time than the original mistake.

Better approach: Check the dedicated item page, the Missable Trinkets guide, and the Black Market guide before restarting.

8Attempting 100% Cleanup From Memory

A large completion run contains too many small objectives to track reliably in your head. Players often revisit the same rooms multiple times because they cannot remember whether a Trinket, boss, quest, or upgrade was already completed.

This makes the final few missing items feel much harder than they actually are.

Better approach: Use the 100% Checklist & Progress Tracker and mark progress as soon as an objective is completed.

9Comparing Runs Without Checking Modifiers

NG+, accessibility options, and gameplay modifiers can change combat, movement, resources, equipment access, and checkpoints. Two players can describe different behavior and both be correct within their own settings.

This becomes a problem when a strategy is copied from a modified run into a standard playthrough.

Better approach: Before following a build or route, confirm the active preset and any important modifiers. See the Hidden Mechanics guide for more context.

10Treating Community Advice as Confirmed Fact

Early guides, forum replies, and comments can mix real mechanics with assumptions, patch-specific behavior, or modifier effects. A confident explanation is not automatically a tested explanation.

This matters most for exact percentages, hidden conditions, item counts, and claims that something is permanently missable.

Better approach: Verify important claims using multiple attempts, current sources, and controlled testing. Change one variable at a time.

Common Mistakes FAQ

Should Sidearms be saved only for bosses?

No. Sidearms can be valuable during exploration. The better rule is to spend Joules intentionally and avoid reaching a difficult room with no resource left.

Is one strong build enough for the whole game?

A general build can handle much of the game, but changing a few Trinkets for bosses, platforming, or cleanup usually makes progress easier.

Does missing one item always require a new save?

No. Recovery depends on the specific item and its condition. Check the relevant item page before restarting.

What is the easiest way to avoid cleanup confusion?

Use a checklist and mark objectives immediately after completing them. Waiting until the end creates unnecessary uncertainty.

Why do guides sometimes disagree?

They may be based on different patches, modifiers, equipment, progression states, or incomplete testing.
