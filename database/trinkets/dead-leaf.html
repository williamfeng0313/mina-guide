Dead Leaf

wilds

Quick Answer: Dead Leaf

Dead Leaf is a utility Trinket in Mina the Hollower. It lets Mina hold still and turn into a silent statue, but the effect breaks when you move. It is more useful for stealth, waiting, and avoiding attention than for direct combat.

If you are collecting every Trinket or checking final completion progress, use this page together with the All Trinket Locations, Missable Trinkets, and 100% Cleanup Checklist.

Effect: Hold still with this leaf to turn into a silent statue. Breaks if you move.

📍 Location: Western Wilds — Reward from Leaf.

Is Dead Leaf Worth Using?

Dead Leaf is worth using when you want a safer way to pause, wait out danger, or avoid enemy attention. It is not a damage Trinket, and it will not help much if you are constantly moving or rushing through rooms.

For 100% cleanup, Dead Leaf is mostly important as a collection check. Make sure it is collected and marked off before assuming your missing Trinket is tied to a shop, boss, or Black Market route.

Best Uses for Dead Leaf

  • Waiting safely when you need enemy movement to settle.
  • Stealth-focused exploration where standing still is possible.
  • Careful cleanup routes where avoiding unnecessary fights is better than rushing.
  • Players who prefer utility Trinkets over pure damage setups.
  • Marking off Western Wilds collection progress during 100% completion.

Dead Leaf and 100% Cleanup

Dead Leaf should be checked during your Western Wilds sweep. If you are using a completion checklist, mark it off only after confirming the Trinket is actually in your collection.

If Dead Leaf is missing, compare your save against the All Trinket Locations guide before assuming it is permanently missed.
