Intravenous Vial

ossex

Quick Answer: Intravenous Vial

Intravenous Vial is a Trinket that automatically uses a Vial when Mina has enough Plasma, removing the need to manually drink one.

You can buy Intravenous Vial from Lady Susan at the Trinket Bazaar in Ossex for 1,250 Bones after collecting at least 5 Trinkets.

Intravenous Vial Details

  • Effect: Automatically use a Vial when Mina has enough Plasma.
  • Location: Ossex — Trinket Bazaar.
  • How to Get: Purchase it from Lady Susan.
  • Unlock Requirement: Collect at least 5 Trinkets.
  • Purchase Price: 1,250 Bones.

How to Get Intravenous Vial

  1. Reach Ossex and visit Lady Susan's Trinket Bazaar.
  2. Continue collecting Trinkets until Mina has at least 5.
  3. Return to Lady Susan and check the Trinket Bazaar inventory.
  4. Purchase Intravenous Vial for 1,250 Bones.

How Does Intravenous Vial Work?

Intravenous Vial automates Vial use once Mina has enough Plasma. Instead of manually stopping to drink, the healing effect is triggered automatically.

This makes it a convenience and survival-focused Trinket, especially in fast encounters where manually timing a Vial can be risky.

Intravenous Vial and Warding Beastium

Intravenous Vial has a notable interaction with Warding Beastium. Current game documentation notes that equipping Intravenous Vial nullifies Warding Beastium's healing penalty.

That makes the combination worth considering for defensive builds that want Warding Beastium's damage reduction without accepting its normal healing drawback.

Is Intravenous Vial Worth Using?

Intravenous Vial is most useful for players who value automatic recovery and do not want to manually time Vial use during fast combat.

Its value increases further in defensive builds, especially when paired with Trinkets that interact with healing or damage reduction.

Best Uses for Intravenous Vial

  • Fast boss fights where manual healing is risky.
  • Defensive and survival-focused builds.
  • Players who prefer automatic recovery.
  • Builds using Warding Beastium.
  • Long exploration or cleanup routes where consistency matters.
