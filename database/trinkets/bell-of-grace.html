Bell of Grace

ossex

Quick Answer: Bell of Grace

Bell of Grace is a utility Trinket in Mina the Hollower that helps reduce death penalties. It gives Mina a chance to keep Spark on death, and on a sparkless death it can save half or all Bones.

Bell of Grace is found in Ossex as a reward from Frayd. It is especially useful for players who are still learning dangerous rooms, bosses, Spark recovery, or late-game cleanup routes.

Effect: Chance to keep Spark on death. On sparkless death, save half or all Bones.

📍 Location: Ossex — Reward from Frayd after completing his 1,000 Bones questline.

Is Bell of Grace Worth Using?

Bell of Grace is worth using if you often die while carrying Bones or struggle to recover Spark safely. It does not increase damage, but it can make difficult exploration, boss learning, and risky cleanup routes less punishing.

For confident players, it may feel less important than damage or movement Trinkets. For first clears and cleanup, it can be valuable because it protects progress when mistakes happen.

Best Uses for Bell of Grace

  • Reducing the risk of losing Bones after repeated deaths.
  • Learning difficult bosses or dangerous rooms.
  • Protecting progress during Spark recovery confusion.
  • Safer exploration when carrying a large number of Bones.
  • Early and mid-game survival routes before you feel confident.

Bell of Grace and 100% Cleanup

Bell of Grace helps indirectly during 100% cleanup by making mistakes less expensive. If you are revisiting old areas, checking missing Trinkets, or moving through dangerous routes with many Bones, it can make cleanup less stressful.

It is not required for completion, but it is useful if you are still learning Spark recovery or want extra protection while backtracking.
